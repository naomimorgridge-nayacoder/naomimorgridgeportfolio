The Thesis

"Complex products need more than good technology. They need a clear market story."

A product can be technically strong and still be hard to buy if the market does not understand what changed, why it matters, why now, and why it should be trusted. That is where I do my best work. I sit between product, market, and revenue, turning technical value into messaging, campaigns, sales materials, buyer education, and commercial narratives that make the product easier to understand and easier to sell.

Severe thunderstorms are one of the most expensive catastrophe perils for U.S. insurers. Verisk had built a model to help the market understand that risk more clearly. My work was to build the GTM strategy around it.

Organisation
Verisk · CRS
Scope
End-to-end · 1+ year
Audience
U.S. insurers, reinsurers
Recognition
Insurance Insider ILS Modelling Initiative of the Year

Severe thunderstorms are frequent, localised, and difficult to forecast, which makes them hard to price, model, and manage across a portfolio. The product had strong technical value, but the market still needed the story.

Insurers needed to understand what made the model different, why the peril required more attention, and how it could support better portfolio decisions. Competitors were also entering the space, which made the positioning window important.

This was not just a launch. It was a sustained market education and adoption campaign across messaging, content, webinars, email, LinkedIn, sales enablement, and internal alignment over 12+ months.

The work needed to be clear, consistent, and commercially useful across every channel where the model showed up.

Property catastrophe modelling is a mature market. Casualty CAT analytics is not. Many buyers were still learning how to think about liability accumulation and the role of analytics in understanding casualty risk. The work was not only about promoting Arium. It was also about helping the market understand the category.

Organisation
Verisk · Arium
Scope
End-to-end · 2 years
Audience
Casualty underwriters, reinsurers
My Role
GTM, positioning, web, content, events, sales enablement

Property CAT buyers have decades of modelling history and a shared vocabulary. Casualty CAT buyers are being asked to quantify something they have always handled through experience: how their liability and workers comp books accumulate exposure to a single large event. The category was still forming.

Arium needed a clearer commercial voice inside Verisk's broader casualty analytics story. The product was being repositioned, the web presence needed work, and the audience needed more education around why casualty CAT mattered.

The challenge was to support an evolving product while building trust with existing users and introducing the story to a wider market.

"The work was not only about promoting Arium. It was also about helping the market understand the category."
When I Started
Strong product, no unified GTM, no buyer-facing narrative, and no event presence. The market category was still forming. Most buyers did not yet have the vocabulary for what Arium offered.
What I Built
Rebuilt the value proposition and messaging. Redesigned the web presence. Built and ran content, email, LinkedIn, and digital demand generation. Supported Liability Symposium London as the flagship executive event. Two years of consistent execution while the product was also evolving.
Two Years Later
Client base grew consistently. Mailing list scaled significantly. Strong content reach across casualty analytics posts. Arium pages now among the most-viewed in CRS. The market moved.

Rebuilt the commercial narrative for Arium, including the value proposition, audience definition, key messages, and how it should be explained to different buyer groups.

Content Strategy & Creation
Led creation of all content across the product lifecycle: thought leadership, technical explainers, buyer education, webinar scripts, email sequences, and sales collateral.
Demand Generation
